Helping Out

Issue 1 - May / June 2013
The bi-monthly newsletter for Reading Pride’s supporters, volunteers and prospective volunteers.

Hello

… and welcome to the first issue of Reading Pride’s newsletter for anyone interested in ‘helping out’ in the run-up to our 10th annual LGBTA Pride parade and festival.

As Volunteer Coordinator on the Reading Pride Committee my role is to find and recruit volunteers to get involved in organising, promoting and supporting Reading Pride, either in the coming weeks, or on the day of the event.

I have volunteered for various charities over the years and many of my happiest memories, proudest achievements and closest friendships are a result of doing so. I want to ensure that everyone volunteering for Reading Pride derives the same level of satisfaction and enjoyment from volunteering that I have (and still do), so finding you a role that you have the skills and time for, and that you enjoy, is paramount to me.

I do however appreciate that for some people, giving up even a couple of hours to volunteer might be impractical because of other commitments. Under those circumstances, you might think that you can’t help us in any way, but actually you can! In every newsletter I will be highlighting particular items and services that we need to source, and you just might know someone, or somewhere, that could provide what we need; all you need to commit is the time it takes to email me your suggestion!

The purpose of this newsletter is therefore twofold – to let you know about some of the volunteering opportunities available and to highlight other, simple ways that you can support us as we gear up for the ‘big day’.

Thanks for supporting Reading Pride – please read on for ideas on how you can continue to do so, and I hope to hear from you soon!

Fran

Volunteer Coordinator, Fran Light, at Reading Pride 2012

Save the date!

This year’s special 10th anniversary Reading Pride is on Saturday 31st August. The parade will start at Reading Civic Centre at 11.30am. The festival site at King’s Meadow Park will open at 12 noon.

This year we will have a separate cabaret tent and a beauty bar offering fast-track beauty treatments alongside our established attractions such as the main stage, market stalls, fun fair and youth zone.

You’re interested in volunteering? That’s great! The first thing you need to do is complete the volunteer application form so that we can get an idea of your skills, how much time you have available and which roles you are interested in. The form can be downloaded from our website; or drop us a request by email address at volunteers@readingpride.co.uk. Once we receive your form, we’ll be in touch to discuss ways that you could help make Reading Pride 2013 a success.

Reading Pride is run entirely by volunteers and as you can imagine, lots of volunteers are needed to plan, promote, publicise and manage a large-scale outdoor event of this kind. Whatever your interests and abilities, and however much, or little, time you can commit, we’d love to have you on our team. Here’s an overview of some of the roles currently available:

Health & Safety Officer

– URGENT!

We need a meticulous individual with an eye for details and relevant health & safety qualifications and experience to take on this important role. You would need to attend some committee meetings and be available in the week before the festival and on the day to conduct on-site inspections.

On-site Team

If you’re reliable, enthusiastic and free on the day of the festival you could provide essential support to Reading Pride’s organisers and the thousands of people who visit throughout the day by volunteering for one of the following roles:

  • Festival Steward
  • Parade Marshal
  • Information Stall Team Member
  • Goody Bag Distributor
  • Donation Collector
  • Site Clean-up Team Member

Help on-site is also required in the days before and after the event; volunteer for whichever day suits you best!

Sponsorship Officers

If you have experience of liaising with potential sponsors or business partners, enjoy being part of important business negotiations and have good letter writing skills, you are just the kind of person we are looking for to join our Sponsorship Team.

Office Support

Individuals with a working knowledge of Word, Excel and/or Power Point and sound business writing skills are required to provide behind the scenes support to our operational teams.

Stage Managers

Organisational, planning and interpersonal skills are essential for the roles of Main Stage Manager and an Assistant Cabaret Stage Manager. Previous experience working closely with performers or in a similar role is preferred. You would need to be available on the day of the festival and for at least a couple of hours a week in the fortnight prior.

These are just some of the opportunities available - a full list of current volunteering vacancies can be downloaded from our website. This list is regularly updated so keep popping back to the website see what new roles have been added since your last visit.

Put up our ‘Save the Date’ publicity poster?

We want to see gyms, colleges, community centres, supermarkets and LGBTA pubs and clubs throughout the Thames Valley displaying our publicity poster. The poster will shortly be available to download from our website and we’d be grateful if you could print out a few copies, take them around your local area and help us to spread the word. Please ask permission from the venue concerned beforehand, we don’t want you to get into any trouble on our behalf!

Spot a good trading opportunity when they see it?

If you’re out and about at other festivals or events this summer please keep a look out for vendors that you think would be a good fit for our Market Place trading area. It might be a commercial company selling anything from wigs to wedding cakes, or a charity sympathetic to LGBTA issues, but if you think they’d go down well, note down the stallholder’s details (or ask for their business card) and pass that information on to us so we can make ‘first contact’.

Donate merchandise to go inside our festival goody bags?

We’re open to creative suggestions from interested companies, but we’re currently thinking along the lines of pens, bottled water, notepads, key-rings and stress toys.

Provide printing services to us free of charge or at greatly reduced rates?

We are planning a big poster and leaflet campaign, and support from a local printing company would really help us out.

Tell us what you think

We are always open to suggestions and happy to answer any questions you have about volunteering or about Reading Pride. What else would you like to see in this newsletter? As a volunteer, what kind of support and information do you want to receive? Reading Pride is your local LGBTA Pride, and this is your Pride newsletter so e-mail us, and tell us what you’d like to see in future issues.
